Leaf Disease Detection (Deep Learning)

A deep learning research project focused on the automated classification of tomato leaf diseases using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). This work was published in a peer-reviewed journal, validating its contribution to the field of agricultural AI.


Key Technical Highlights:

  • Dataset & Preprocessing: Trained and validated on a large-scale dataset of 15,000+ annotated plant leaf images. Applied augmentation techniques including flipping, rotation, and contrast adjustment to improve model generalization.
  • CNN Architecture: Designed and optimized a multi-layer CNN with convolutional, pooling, batch normalization, and dropout layers to identify 10+ disease categories with high accuracy.
  • Model Performance: Achieved strong classification accuracy through iterative training and hyperparameter tuning, significantly outperforming baseline approaches.
  • Framework & Tools: Built entirely using Python, TensorFlow, and Keras. Evaluation metrics included accuracy, precision, recall, and confusion matrix analysis.
  • Published Research: The findings and methodology were documented and published in a peer-reviewed academic journal, affirming the scientific rigor and reproducibility of the approach.
